Creatures

The Pacific Ocean is home to diverse marine life, including whales, sharks, jellyfish, coral reefs, and more.

Depth

The average depth of the Pacific Ocean is about 12,080 feet (3,682 meters), with the Mariana Trench reaching 36,037 feet (10,994 meters).

Area

The Pacific Ocean spans approximately 63 million square miles (165 million square kilometers), making it the largest ocean on Earth.

Danger Zones

  • The "Ring of Fire" with its active volcanoes and earthquakes.
  • Powerful tsunamis and hurricanes.

Special Features

The Pacific Ocean is famous for its vast coral reefs, such as the Great Barrier Reef, and unique island chains like Hawaii.
